OpenZ: 1/28 scale RWD mini RC car by modminiman on Thingiverse: https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:4463020

Summary:
A highly modified version of the OpenZ frames currently shared by Guaro3d (https://www.guaro3d.com/openz) as well as Rockracer (https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:4360727).I printed both the deisgns above but wanted to tighten up the steering and other issues. One thing led to another and by the end I had changed every single part. Note that parts are mostly NOT INTERCHANGEABLE with the above 2 designs.I made changes mostly to fit my own needs/capabilities of my printers/availability of specific items. This might not work for everyone, especially the ball hubs and ball links, not sure if an FDM printer can print this in the tolerances needed.Changes made include:Front End:Front plate modified to just use M2 screws for everythingFront suspension using rubber band (see photos)Front arms and wheel hubs printed with ball connectorsFront steering using printed ball linksMain Chassis:Main chassis widened to accommodate my specific lipo (might have issues fitting shells)Top brace split into front and main to accommodate taller servo hornRear Suspension:Spring based suspension using spring from a pen and an M3 screwRear End:Beefed up the trailing arm with M3 instead of M2 screwsModified motor housing to account for gear mesh with 12T pinionModified motor housing to fit brushless motor (Atomic 5500kv used here, others might not fit)Wheels:Tires and rims all redesigned, tires printed by FDM in PLA+, rims were printed by SLATire remover STLs included (don't work super well but better than nothing)Rear wheels are made of 2 pieces, hub and rim to allow for installation of M2 screw to axleRH rear hub is screwed directly to a 40T spur gearBody Shells:I have included 2, one generic just to let people know where to place the magnet posts and another based on Guardo's Dodge Challenger (https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:2861048) which was scaled and edited to include the posts for magnets. His design also was nicely split into easily printable sections.Outstanding issues:Still not happy with the front steeringCan't get any more extreme steering angles(EDIT: with the latest dual servo horns and some other changes this seems ENOUGH, not as extreme as off the shelf solutions but enough)Steering affected by suspension(EDIT: this has mainly been resolved by the point below, also using the Front Plate with Arm Stop helps to prevent the arms from over extending downwards and allows for a stronger rubber band to be used for the front suspension)Steering pumps at maximum travel(EDIT: this has been reduced by using the dual servo arm and offset top arms and arm holder, also swapping the left and right hubs (3mm with 2mm caster) so that the push rods are flatter)printed servo ball links not ideal, recommend buying proper M2 ones(EDIT: I bought M2 ball links but they are the same size if not BIGGER than my printed ones!)Low speed controlNo idea if this is an ESC problem, a drive train problem, a tire problem or just me being new at drifting, I can't get the car to operate at a smooth low speed for smooth drifts(EDIT: This has been partially resolved for me in part by moving from 12T/40T to 9T/43T as well as swapping out the flat profile back tires with the rounded profile ones)Rear axle issuesCurrently either friction of glue to adapt the axle to the rear hubs, sometimes slipsMy current axle DOES NOT FIT the MR63ZZ bearings!
